Qwan Ki Do

Qwan Ki Do or Quan khi Dao is a Vietnamese martial art that was codified in France in 1981. Qwan Ki Do is practiced
internationally, schools in Asia and Europe. The practice combines the use of hand-to-hand techniques and weapons,
with moves combined in both formalised combinations, termed Thao Quyen, and freeform settings. Qwan Ki Do includes
stances, punches, kicks, throws, holds and many other techniques. Weapon moves include the traditional sword art of
Viet Lon Guom. Practitioners can progress through three levels, called So Dang, Trung Dang and Thuong Dang or Nhap
Man, Trung Man and Dai Man, at which different coloured belts are worn. As well as improving physical attributes like
strength and flexibility in adults, the practice has been shown to help promote good mental health in children.
